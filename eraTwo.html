Welcome to the Era Empress (2018 - 2020)

Never before had the Empress despaired to the extent that she did these days. There were landscapes to traverse and time to maneuver. All around, disasters occurred. In the pinhole of lightness she fumbled for tools, tools that had eluded her but gradually fell into step.




UP TO OUR THEN AND NOW
Ancestor quilt, hand sewn with found fabrics, 96"h x 65"w, 2020.
The girl sits by the phone half-expectantly half-dejectedly and receives eight phone calls from various members of her bloodline. They instruct her to do things, ie. put on an energy suit to protect her from unresolved forces in the world, write her name in ink so she can remember who she is, eat a peach for longevity and good health. Eventually she learns how to pay respects to her forebears and greet her guests, the audience members standing outside the window. Though separated by a barrier, they figuratively share cups of tea that she ceremonially pours and presents to them.

A laptop screen shows text being typed out, narrating her actions and reactions. Who is this narrator?

In the final scene, she receives a call from her parents, who remind her to rest every once in a while. She cuts the phone cord and lies down to sleep.

Longing for ancestors is like waiting for a phone call that only comes disguised as mundane chats with friends. Longing for the ones who deeply understand where I come from is only released when I speak and am soothed by my existence in its fullest. Many thanks to Marcia and Jeremy of Maelstrom Collaborative Arts and everybody who came out to see the show. Thanks also to Haumed Rahmani for recording video and Kaitlin K. Walsh for the photo documentation. Developed in collaboration with Jungmok Yi as yFFy.

EAR PIECE
Performed on September 27, 2018 at Pratt Institute. This piece emerged from an imaginative investigation of the ear. Namely, the ear goes on a journey through its own anatomical landscape in order to find itself. In the first section, she encounters the lobe. Then, she reaches the eardrum which emits a deafening screech. Following that she spends a quiet moment with the tiny bones of the middle ear, before moving on to the semicircular canals and cochlea where she wobbles and works through her sense of balance.

PUPPET
A puppet made of hand-carved bass wood, sculpted thermoplastic beads and epoxy, leather, wire, and fabric. Figurehead of an investigation of gestures and the making of them - anatomically, mechanically, emotionally. Music, motion, and language layered in a web of expressive gesture.

References and exercises on gesture.

CATERPILLAR GROW-TIME
I reimagined the classic Snake game as a caterpillar's growth journey, where each leaf eaten triggers a random bird out of 5 types to appear as an obstacle on screen. The objective is to eat without being eaten. Each bird announces its presence with a species-specific bird sound. This game is a prototype for a suite of educational nature-based games.

Sounds from Audubon.
Written in C++/openFrameworks.

YARN STORY
I met the Empress Farmhand in the stormy depths. She guided my fingers to be like whispers around wisps of fiber as they vortexed toward the eyelet of the wheel that spun. The fibers, once unruly, became organized. Bundled up, I shared the yarns with friends and they sent messages back to me.

Yarn spun and sent to Marcela in Carmel, NY.
The letter that accompanied the yarn.
Marcela's letter in response.

I arranged the yarns into planes of loops, which are just orderly entanglements. They called for the body, so I knit them into socks, which evolved into a temple.
