Hand woven 100% Merino wool (Jaggerspun lace-weight 18/2), 5.5 yds, 2/2 twill. I sought to create an organic plaid, whereby the color repetition is consistent (mirrored sequence of burgundy-red-sage-green-lavender-magenta) but the thicknesses of the bands vary according to the weaver's intuition. I wound the warp, dressed the loom, and began weaving in December 2019. In March 2020, the pandemic shutdown forced me to leave the loom for two months. In July, I resumed weaving, and finished the full warp length in November 2020. The elasticity of the wool warp caused the fabric to lengthen significantly beyond the initial warp length of 6 yards. The warp was also subject to snapping, especially at the edges where there was heightened strain on the threads. Near the end, I felt constricted by the color sequence and experimented with dropping colors or disrupting the sequence to create new variations of the fabric.
Specs: 12 EPI, approx. 52" wide
